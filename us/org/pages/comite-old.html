BitBurger Works Council

The BitBurger Works Council has been formally established to represent all employees of the company. Its current composition reflects a proportional distribution among the different departments and functional areas, ensuring equitable representation.

Constitution and Composition of the Committee

The main leadership structure is as follows:

  • President: Mr. Andrew Calveche
  • Secretary: Mr. Alvaro López
  • Member: Mr. Arturo Marco
  • Member: Ms. Lucía Fernández
  • Member: Ms. Ana Belmonte

These 5 representatives act on behalf of the diverse functional areas of the company, ensuring the advocacy of interests for the entire workforce. The commissions involving these employee representatives are:

Committee Commissions

Health and Safety Commission

Responsible: Andrew Calveche

Objectives:

  • Ensure safe and healthy working conditions for all staff, promoting a preventative culture within the company.
  • Oversee compliance with current occupational risk prevention regulations.
  • Encourage worker participation in improving safety and health conditions.

Main functions:

  • Actively collaborate with the company's risk prevention service in identifying, assessing, and controlling occupational hazards.
  • Monitor compliance with preventive measures and propose continuous improvements in occupational health and ergonomics.
  • Supervise and be consulted on risk assessments, planning of preventive activities, emergency and evacuation plans, and the selection and suitability of personal protective equipment (PPE).
  • Collect, analyze, and channel worker complaints, suggestions, and initiatives regarding unsafe or unhealthy working conditions.
  • Participate in the investigation of occupational accidents and incidents to identify causes and propose corrective measures.
  • Promote training and information programs on risk prevention for workers.
  • Conduct periodic visits to various workstations to verify safety and health conditions.

Equality Commission

Responsible: Lucía Fernández

Objectives:

  • Promote and ensure equal treatment and opportunities for women and men in all areas of the company.
  • Eliminate any form of direct or indirect discrimination based on sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other personal or social condition.
  • Ensure the correct implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of the company's Equality Plan.
  • Foster a work environment free from sexual and gender-based harassment.

Main functions:

  • Actively participate in the diagnosis, design, negotiation, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of Bitburger's Equality Plan.
  • Monitor the effective application of measures contained in the Equality Plan in areas such as access to employment, professional classification, promotion, training, remuneration, working time arrangements to promote conciliation, and harassment prevention.
  • Analyze and propose measures to correct possible wage inequalities (gender pay gap) and ensure the principle of 'equal pay for work of equal value'.
  • Receive and channel inquiries, complaints, or reports related to situations of discrimination or harassment, ensuring confidentiality and due process according to established protocols.
  • Promote awareness and training actions on equal opportunities and harassment prevention for all staff.
  • Ensure the use of inclusive and non-sexist language in all internal and external company communications.
  • Collaborate in the preparation of periodic reports on the progress of equality policies.

Training Commission

Responsible: Arturo Marco and Ana Belmonte

Objectives:

  • Foster the professional and personal development of staff through continuous training.
  • Ensure that the training offered is relevant, of high quality, and responds to both the company's needs and the workers' aspirations.
  • Promote employee adaptability to technological and organizational changes, improving their employability.
  • Ensure equal access to training opportunities for all groups within the company.

Main functions:

  • Participate in identifying training needs at the individual, departmental, and company-wide levels.
  • Collaborate in the development, monitoring, and evaluation of the company's annual Training Plan, issuing a report prior to its approval.
  • Inform and guide workers on the different training actions available (internal, external, subsidized training, individual training leave, company training grants).
  • Propose new topics, methodologies, and training actions considered beneficial for the staff and the company.
  • Ensure that selection criteria for access to training are objective and non-discriminatory.
  • Evaluate the impact and effectiveness of the training actions carried out, proposing improvements if necessary.
  • Encourage worker participation in skills and competency development programs.

Economic and Budgetary Control Commission

Responsible: Alvaro López

Objectives:

  • Ensure transparency in the company's economic and financial information that affects workers' interests.
  • Supervise the correct application of agreed economic accords and budgetary allocations for labor and social aspects.
  • Analyze the company's economic situation and its impact on employment and working conditions.

Main functions:

  • Receive, analyze, and issue reports on the economic information that Management must provide to the Works Council according to Article 64 of the Workers' Statute (ET) (general evolution of the economic sector, company's economic situation, activity forecasts, probable evolution of employment, etc.).
  • Supervise compliance with wage commitments, including agreed increases, supplements, and the correct application of collective agreement salary scales.
  • Monitor the execution of budgets allocated to specific items benefiting workers, such as funds for social benefits, training (in coordination with the training commission), or equality plans.
  • Be consulted and report on business decisions with significant economic impact on the workforce (e.g., restructuring, substantial modifications of working conditions with an economic basis).
  • Analyze quarterly information on the general progress of the company (mentioned in section 7 "Right to Economic Information") and convey relevant conclusions to the workforce in an understandable manner.
  • Propose, where appropriate, measures that can contribute to improving efficiency or optimizing resources, always for the benefit of maintaining and improving working conditions.
