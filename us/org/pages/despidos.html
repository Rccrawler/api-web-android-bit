Reasons for Which You Can Be Dismissed

At BitBurger, we value our team. However, there are certain behaviors and situations that, in accordance with current labor legislation and our internal policies, can lead to the termination of the employment contract. It is important to be aware of them:

Disciplinary Dismissal (Art. 54 Workers' Statute)

  • Repeated and unjustified absences or lateness.
  • Indiscipline or disobedience at work.
  • Verbal or physical offenses against the employer, colleagues, or their families.
  • Breach of contractual good faith and abuse of trust.
  • Continuous and voluntary decrease in normal performance.
  • Habitual drunkenness or drug addiction if they negatively affect work.
  • Harassment based on racial or ethnic origin, religion, disability, age, or sexual orientation, and sexual harassment or harassment based on sex.

Dismissal for Objective Reasons (Art. 52 Workers' Statute)

  • Worker's incompetence known or arising after effective placement.
  • Worker's lack of adaptation to technical modifications made to their job, when such changes are reasonable and at least two months have passed since the modification.
  • Economic, technical, organizational, or production reasons (collective or individual dismissal for these reasons).
  • Absences from work, even if justified but intermittent, reaching 20% of working days in two consecutive months (with certain limits and exclusions).

Important Note: This is a general list. Each case is evaluated individually, and established legal procedures will always be followed, guaranteeing the worker's right to be heard. For more details, consult the Workers' Statute and the applicable Collective Bargaining Agreement.

Company-Specific Dismissal Criteria (Optional)

At BitBurger, in addition to legally established reasons, the following are especially considered for continued employment and development within the company:

  • Commitment to Quality and the Customer: Excellence in service and the quality of our products are paramount. Serious or repeated failures in this aspect are grounds for serious consideration.
  • Teamwork and Respect: We foster a collaborative environment. Attitudes that deteriorate the work climate or show disrespect to colleagues or customers are not tolerated.
  • Compliance with Internal Regulations: Hygiene, food safety policies, and operational regulations are mandatory. Serious non-compliance can affect the employment relationship.
  • Proactivity and Continuous Improvement: We value initiative and the desire to improve. Passivity or constant resistance to change without justification may be evaluated negatively in the long term.

These criteria are evaluated in the context of overall performance and always under the principles of fairness and proportionality. Transparent communication and opportunities for improvement are part of our process.

Detailed Legal Framework and References

Key Articles of the Workers' Statute on Dismissals

  • Article 51: Regulates collective dismissal, establishing the conditions and procedures for its application.
  • Article 52: Details the causes for contract termination due to objective reasons, such as worker incompetence or economic causes.
  • Article 53: Establishes the form and effects of termination for objective reasons.
  • Article 54: Defines disciplinary dismissal, specifying the causes that justify it.
  • Article 55: Describes the form and effects of disciplinary dismissal.
  • Article 56: Regulates the consequences of unfair dismissal, including the option of reinstatement or compensation.
